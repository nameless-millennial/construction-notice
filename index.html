Project Overview

A modest second story above an existing home

The project adds a new second story to the existing building and renovates the ground floor. No new dwelling units are created — the property remains a three-unit, owner-occupied residence with its existing parking retained.

Project description. The proposal is the addition of a second story to the existing structure, together with a ground-floor renovation. The building currently contains three dwelling units and will continue to contain three dwelling units after construction — the scope adds floor area only, not units. The property is entirely owner-occupied with no tenants.

Architectural character. The addition is composed as a quiet, contemporary volume — warm vertical oak slat siding and dark-bronze metal paired with smooth integral-color stucco, a charcoal shingle roof, steel-framed window boxes, and a planted roof deck screened by frameless glass and slim steel rail.

Scope & areas. Ground-floor renovation of units 1147 and 1149, plus a 1,883 SF second-floor addition serving both units — with new exterior materials, doors, windows, roofing, electrical, plumbing, kitchens, lighting, and interior finishes throughout. A third dwelling unit is located above the detached garage. Building areas per the architect’s tabulation: Unit 1147 — 2,302.5 SF; Unit 1149 — 1,296 SF.

Materials & Palette

Warm, durable, residential materials
